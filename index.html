My work process

For me, working on a design project, no matter if it's a simple website or a more complex system, if it is just a flyer or a large brochure, or if it will be a whole brand identity, always starts in one place - a discussion. I want to get familiar with the client's business, goals, and specifics, who the competitors are... Some of the other questions of high importance are:

  • What makes the client's business better than the rest? Why does it matter, and why do people have to choose it, not something else?
  • What is the target user profile - who are they, what do they want and need? What are their goals, needs, specifics, and more?
  • Is there any user-related data available?

Based on that data, we can proceed with: generating ideas, making prototypes and drafts, discussing and testing them, and evaluating the results. And then - improver and repeat...

UI/UX Design

Designing a User Interface always comes to one thing - finding the sweet spot between the business goals and the user needs.

Made the proper way, that would require steps like research, ideation, creating different mind maps and journey maps, testing, and evaluation, and several rounds of iteration.

In my work on UI design projects, I have been using tools like Miro, Figma, Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop.

Logo Design

As with any design project, the Logo design projects are no different in terms of the work process.

    Each project starts with:
  • a brief about the business;
  • discussion who are the competitors and how they present themselves;
  • what is the look and feel we must achieve;
  • where will the logo be used primarily and more.

Based on that, some quick drafts are in order (digital and/or hand-drawn), discussions and edits are made until we get to the desired look.

Print Layout

Each print layout design project starts with the content and the requirements of the printing technique that will be used depending on the final product, print media, and the print copies of the edition.

In most of the projects of that kind, I have used at least two tools of the "holy trinity": Adobe InDesign (for designs with a large number of pages), Adobe Illustrator (for small projects like flyers and leaflets), and Adobe Photoshop (for photo editing).
